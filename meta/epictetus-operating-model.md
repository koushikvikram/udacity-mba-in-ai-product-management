# Epictetus Operating Model — Mind Master (Viswanathan Anand)

![Epictetus Operating Model](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: elite performance is the synthesis of *tactical preparation*, *psychological resilience*, and *adaptive learning*. Strategy is just long-term tactics. Memory is contextual (patterns/stories), not photographic. Predictability is the silent long-term hazard. The modern edge is cognitive flexibility — including the willingness to relinquish ego in front of better tools (AI).

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we're trying to achieve

- **Win under pressure**: stay objective, keep playing the position you actually have, not the one you wish you had.
- **Compound mastery**: build pattern memory, refresh it, and emotionally anchor it so it stays.
- **Defeat your own predictability**: keep adding new openings (literal or metaphorical) so opponents can't neutralize you.
- **Adopt better tools without ego**: let the machine teach you when it's right and you're wrong.

### Non-negotiable behaviors

- **Short bursts of deep work**, not marathons.
- **Loss post-mortem within 24 hours** ("the mother's rule") — write the position, the blunder, and the worry while emotions are raw.
- **Pre-commit to discomfort** ("ditch the ladder") at least once a week — an action that forecloses retreat.
- **Objectivity drill** when in trouble: assume the loss has already happened; play the resources that remain.
- **Inscrutability** in high-stakes interactions — control breath, face, tempo.
- **Periodic revision** for any new material (1d / 3d / 1w / 1m).
- **AI as mentor**: routinely consult a tool whose conclusion you might disagree with; accept at least one such conclusion despite ego resistance.

### Routines / checklists

- **Daily short bursts (1–3 × 25–50 min)**
  - One problem.
  - All distractions blocked.
  - One concrete artifact at the end (notes, a decision, a snippet, a fix).

- **Periodic revision schedule (per new pattern / concept / template)**
  - Same day → +1 day → +3 days → +1 week → +1 month.
  - Add an **emotional anchor**: the loss / embarrassment / surprise that made it salient.

- **Loss / setback post-mortem (within 24 hours)**
  - Reconstruct the "position" before the mistake.
  - Name the blunder clearly (no euphemism).
  - Identify the *pattern* it belongs to (not just this instance).
  - One adjustment to test next time — small enough to actually do.

- **"Ditch the ladder" rep (≥ 1 / week)**
  - Pick a strong-but-uncomfortable move (a hard email, a public commitment, an opening you don't own yet, a pricing change).
  - Lock in retreat-prevention (publish, schedule, ship, announce).
  - Note: the discomfort is the rep.

- **Objectivity drill (when stakes look dire)**
  - One line: *"Assume I've already lost. What resources still exist?"*
  - Play the remaining position. Don't litigate the past five moves.

- **Inscrutability rep (≥ 1 high-stakes interaction / week)**
  - Steady breath, neutral face, deliberate tempo.
  - Notice your tells; reduce them by one this week.

- **AI co-evaluator (per major decision)**
  - Pose the decision to a tool / model.
  - Document one conclusion you accepted *despite* ego resistance.

- **Energy-conservation routine (pre-event)**
  - Identical sleep window, meal, clothing, arrival time, warm-up.
  - The point: spend cognition on the **event**, not the setup.

### What good execution looks like

- Post-mortems are routine and fast — losses become inputs, not wounds.
- You consistently choose the uncomfortable-but-better move over the comfortable-but-readable one.
- You catch yourself *replaying* the past during a hard moment, and re-anchor on the position in front of you.
- You let a tool overrule you on at least one decision per week without resentment.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Strategy is long-term tactics**: execution quality compounds; a brilliant strategy with weak tactics still loses.
- **Memory is a story, not a snapshot**: patterns + emotional anchors + revision = retention; raw effort doesn't.
- **Objectivity > rumination**: assuming the worst case has happened removes the anxiety of the *what-if* and frees attention for the *what now*.
- **Predictability is the long-term hazard**: at the elite level, the exact thing that made you win becomes the thing they neutralize. Controlled risk is the antidote to the plateau.
- **Ego loses to AI**: the differentiator is no longer hardware; it is the willingness to accept a conclusion that contradicts your intuition.
- **Routine is energy management**: the more decisions you make about *the setup*, the fewer you have for *the play*.

### Decision rules (doctrine)

- If you're tempted to skip the post-mortem because the loss "stings" — that *is* the post-mortem worth doing.
- If your last 5 moves (in any domain) look readable, ditch a ladder this week.
- If you're grinding for hours, switch to a short burst with a clear artifact.
- If a tool disagrees with you and you're upset, ask why **before** defending.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

Track in [metrics.md](./metrics.md). Review with [templates/weekly-meta-review.md](./templates/weekly-meta-review.md).

### Metrics (leading, practice-protecting)

- **Patterns mastered / week** (new positions, structures, templates encoded with anchor + revision schedule).
- **Post-mortem rate**: % of losses / setbacks reviewed within 24 hours.
- **Ditched-ladder reps / week**.
- **AI-disagreements accepted / week** (conclusions you took despite ego resistance).
- **Routine compliance** (pre-event ritual hit-rate — protects cognition for the event).
- **Inscrutability reps / week**.

### Review loop

- **Weekly (15 min)**:
  - Where was I most predictable?
  - Which pattern needs another revision pass?
  - Which loss did I avoid post-mortem-ing — and why?
- **Monthly**: change one variable in your "opening repertoire" (literal or metaphorical) so you remain hard to neutralize.

### Core rule guardrail

- If metrics are growing but you stopped doing **post-mortems** and **short bursts**, restore those before adding any new tracking. The two practices are the spine — everything else is decoration.
