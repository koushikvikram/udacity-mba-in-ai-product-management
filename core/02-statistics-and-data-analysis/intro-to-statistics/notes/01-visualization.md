### 2016-06-11 - Looking at Data

### 1. Key Ideas (In Order of Appearance)

#### Data → Decisions

##### Product managers use data to make better decisions

Data helps answer questions such as:

* Which opportunities should we pursue?
* Which solution should we choose?
* Are we creating value?

Data supports judgment rather than replacing it.

---

##### Decisions require interpretation

Raw numbers alone are insufficient.

PMs must:

* Understand what the data means.
* Translate data into business decisions.
* Connect evidence to product goals.

---

##### Product decisions involve uncertainty

Data helps reduce uncertainty but does not eliminate it.

PMs frequently make decisions without perfect information.

---

#### Valuing Houses Exercises

##### People naturally estimate value from comparisons

To determine a house's value, we compare it with similar houses.

Relevant factors include:

* Size
* Location
* Number of rooms
* Other characteristics

---

##### More information improves estimates

Using additional attributes generally leads to better predictions.

Not all characteristics contribute equally.

---

##### Relationships between variables matter

House prices depend on combinations of factors rather than a single variable.

Different attributes influence value differently.

---

##### Predictions are approximations

Valuation involves estimating rather than determining an exact answer.

There is uncertainty in every estimate.

---

##### Models improve through iteration

Predictions can be refined by:

* Adding information.
* Adjusting assumptions.
* Comparing predictions against actual outcomes.

---

### 2. Frameworks, Definitions, and Named Concepts

#### Data-Driven Decision Making

Using evidence to improve product decisions rather than relying solely on intuition.

---

#### Estimation

Producing an approximate answer when exact values are unknown.

---

#### Comparative Valuation

Estimating value by comparing similar items.

---

#### Inputs → Prediction → Outcome

General pattern:

1. Observe characteristics.
2. Estimate value.
3. Compare with reality.
4. Improve future estimates.

---

#### Iterative Improvement

Predictions become better through repeated learning.

---

### 3. Examples and Case Studies

#### House Valuation

Used throughout the lessons.

Students estimate prices based on characteristics of houses.

Purpose:

* Demonstrate how data supports decisions.
* Show that predictions improve with more information.
* Illustrate uncertainty and approximation.

---

#### Successive Exercises

Multiple valuation problems show:

* Initial estimates.
* Revised estimates.
* Improved predictions.

Lesson:

Better information and better models lead to better decisions.

Expected answer:

#### Does data eliminate uncertainty?

No.

Data improves decisions but does not guarantee correctness.

Producing an approximate value using available information.

---

#### Why compare similar houses?

Because comparable characteristics help estimate value.

---

#### Why are predictions imperfect?

Because:

* Information is incomplete.
* Relationships are complex.

#### Why iterate?

Because models improve when predictions are tested and refined.
