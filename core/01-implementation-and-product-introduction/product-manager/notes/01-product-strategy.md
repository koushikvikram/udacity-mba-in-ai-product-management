## Before I start — what I already know

- Product managers define the product vision 1-pager and product strategy.
- Product managers talk to customers to understand their pain points and needs.
- Product managers present prototypes, features and products to customers to get their feedback.
- Product managers track user and product metrics in order to improve the product and decide what features to build.
- Product managers manage many types of risks. [How to Manage Risks as a Product Manager - The Product Compass](https://www.productcompass.pm/p/how-to-manage-risks-as-a-product-manager)
- Product managers are involved in product discovery.
- Product managers communicate user needs and product priorities to a wide variety of stakeholders including Engineering, Design, Marketing and Business.
- It is the Product Manager's job to figure out what to build and what's feasible to build with what budget and what timeline. [Bud Tribble and Steve Jobs describing a Product Manager role in 1986](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_43XPfJEqWc)
- The "JTBD Playbook" says the "Job" is a stable unit for understanding customers.
- "The Product-Minded Engineer" says the "Scenario" is the core primitive of product thinking.
  - A "Scenario" is a user story specifically structured to elicit critical thinking about a product design.

## 2026-06-09 - Introduction to Product Management

### 1. Key Ideas (In Order of Appearance)

#### Product Management 101

##### PMs are often viewed as mini-CEOs

* Own product development, launch, and improvement.
* Core responsibility is identifying and defining problems.

##### PMs need deep understanding of:

* Users
* User needs
* Their own product
* Competitors

##### PMs answer four fundamental questions

###### What?

* What should be built?
* What should the product do?

Products must create value, not just features.

###### Who?

* Understand users deeply.
* Different users have different needs.

###### Why?

* Why solve this problem?
* Why this solution?

Solutions should:

* Create user value.
* Support business objectives.

PMs must explain tradeoffs and decisions.

###### When?

* Prioritize what gets built.
* Decide roadmap timing.
* Accept inevitable tradeoffs.

##### Product management is strategic

Decisions should:

* Support business goals.
* Create a coherent product narrative.
* Connect present work with future opportunities.

##### Great PMs need:

* User awareness
* Product intuition
* Vision
* Adaptability
* Communication skills

PMs act as:

* Source of truth.
* Communicators across audiences.
* Translators between:

  * User needs
  * Design solutions
  * Technical solutions
  * Business impact

---

#### Why Product Management Matters

##### Building products is increasingly difficult

* Easy to build products that fail.
* Hard to build great products.

Teams need someone focused on:

* Users
* Problems
* What truly matters

##### PMs align teams

Alignment produces better product outcomes.

##### Solving problems alone is insufficient

There are infinite problems.

Key question:

> Are we solving the right problem?

##### Products must solve real problems for real users

Not every problem creates a successful product.

##### Products must create value for:

* Users
* The business

Value exchange examples:

* Direct payment
* Advertising

##### A common mistake

Teams build things they personally want.

This may create value for too few users to support a business.

##### PMs connect functions across the company

PMs:

* Advocate for users.
* Understand the business.
* Empower design.
* Create stability for engineering.
* Maintain focus and alignment.

---

#### History of Product Management

##### 1931: Neil McElroy and Procter & Gamble

"Brand Men" memo became a foundation for product management.

Brand managers:

* Monitored sales.
* Advertised products.
* Ran promotions.

Important idea:

> Study things personally through field testing.

##### Hewlett-Packard

Bill Hewlett and David Packard were influenced by McElroy.

HP philosophy:

> Put decision-making close to customers.

Closer to customers → better understanding of problems.

##### Post-WWII Japanese manufacturing

###### Kaizen

"Change for the better."

Continuous improvement through small changes.

Small improvements compound over time.

###### Genchi Genbutsu

"Real location, real thing."

To understand something:

* Go observe reality firsthand.

Benefits:

* Better decisions.
* User empathy.
* Deeper understanding.

##### Product management originally came from marketing

Focus was the 4 Ps:

1. Product
2. Place
3. Price
4. Promotion

Because development cycles were poor, emphasis shifted toward:

* Place
* Price
* Promotion

This role became known as:

> Brand Management

##### Rise of technology and Agile

Technology products required stronger customer focus.

Cross-functional teams became common.

Product managers increasingly focused on:

* User needs
* Defining what products should do

Some companies still share:

* Place
* Price
* Promotion

with marketing teams.

### 6. Compressed Takeaways (My-Note Style)

* PM ≠ feature manager.

  PM = problem finder + value creator + team aligner.

* Four questions drive PM:

  **What → Who → Why → When**

* Solving problems is easy.

  Solving the right problem for enough users is hard.

* Great products create both:

  * User value
  * Business value

* Go to reality.

  Observe users directly.

  *(Genchi Genbutsu)*

* Continuous small improvements compound.

  *(Kaizen)*
